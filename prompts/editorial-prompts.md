# Editorial Prompts

Prompts for drafting, expanding, and refining content in this guide.

---

## Section Drafting

```
You are writing a section for a practitioner's guide to AI in organizational settings.

Section: [SECTION TITLE]
Audience: [e.g., enterprise architects / HR leaders / executives]
Goal: [what the reader should be able to do or decide after reading]

Write in a direct, neutral tone. Avoid hype language.
Include: specific tradeoffs, failure modes, and actionable guidance.
Do not include: marketing language, generic AI claims, or filler text.

Length: [target word count]
```

---

## Content Review

```
Review the following section for:
1. Accuracy — are claims specific and defensible?
2. Tone — is it direct and neutral, not promotional?
3. Completeness — are key tradeoffs and failure modes covered?
4. Actionability — does the reader know what to do?

Flag specific sentences that need revision and explain why.

[PASTE SECTION]
```

---

## Table Generation

```
Generate a comparison table for [TOPIC] with the following columns:
[Column 1] | [Column 2] | [Column 3]

Include [N] rows covering [scope].
Keep entries concise (one phrase per cell).
Do not include a "Recommendation" column — present tradeoffs neutrally.
```

---

## Glossary Entry

```
Write a glossary entry for: [TERM]

Format:
**[Term]**
[2-3 sentence definition. Precise. No marketing language. Include context for why it matters in an organizational AI context.]
```

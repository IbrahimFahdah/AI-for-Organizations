# Employee Adoption

## Overview

Technology adoption is a people problem. AI tools that are technically capable often fail in practice because the people using them do not understand them, do not trust them, or do not have incentives to use them.

---

## Adoption Barriers

### Understanding Gaps

Employees who do not understand what an AI tool does or does not do will either:
- Avoid it (too uncertain)
- Over-rely on it (too trusting)

Neither is the right outcome. Effective training must convey both capability and limitation.

### Trust Deficit

Trust in AI tools is built through:
- Demonstrated accuracy on familiar tasks
- Transparent handling of errors and uncertainty
- Consistent behavior over time

Trust is lost quickly when AI produces confidently wrong outputs without warning.

### Threat Perception

If employees believe an AI tool is intended to replace them, adoption will be low and usage will be strategic rather than genuine. Address this directly:

- Be clear about what the tool is for
- Involve employees in design and piloting
- Connect adoption to outcomes employees care about (less drudgery, more interesting work)

### Workflow Friction

If using the AI tool requires more steps than not using it, people will not use it. Integration should reduce friction, not add it.

---

## Adoption Strategies

### Role-Based Training

Generic AI training is less effective than training specific to an employee's role and tasks. Train on:
- The specific tool being deployed
- The specific tasks it applies to
- The specific errors to watch for in that context

### Champions and Early Adopters

Identify employees who are enthusiastic early adopters. Give them early access, training, and a role in helping peers. Peer-to-peer adoption is more effective than top-down mandates.

### Feedback Loops

Create structured ways for employees to report AI errors and suggest improvements. This serves two purposes:
- Improves the system
- Gives employees agency, which increases buy-in

### Measure and Share Results

Show employees the concrete impact of AI adoption — time saved, errors caught, throughput increased. People are more likely to adopt tools that demonstrably work.

---

## Anti-Patterns

- Mandating adoption without training
- Deploying AI without involving the people whose work it changes
- Measuring adoption by license activation rather than actual use
- Ignoring resistance as a compliance problem rather than a signal

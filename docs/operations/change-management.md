# Change Management

## Overview

AI deployments are organizational changes. The technical component is often the easiest part. Managing the human, process, and cultural change is where most deployments succeed or fail.

---

## Change Management Basics

### Stakeholder Mapping

Identify who is affected by the AI deployment:

| Stakeholder Group | Interest | Concern | Engagement Strategy |
|---|---|---|---|
| Frontline users | Efficiency, ease of use | Job security, learning curve | Early involvement, training |
| Team leads | Productivity metrics | Managing new workflows | Co-design, escalation paths |
| IT/Security | Integration, compliance | Risk, support burden | Technical review, support model |
| Legal/Compliance | Policy alignment | Liability, data risk | Policy input, ongoing review |
| Executive sponsors | ROI, strategic progress | Cost, reputational risk | Regular reporting |

### Communication Planning

Effective communication covers:
- **What** the AI system does and does not do
- **Why** the organization is adopting it
- **What** changes for each affected group
- **How** concerns and feedback will be handled
- **When** key milestones occur

Communication should be specific and honest. Vague positivity ("AI will help you do your job better") is less credible than specifics ("this tool will generate first drafts of these three document types, which currently take 2 hours each").

### Resistance as Signal

Resistance to AI adoption is frequently information, not obstruction:

- Employees may know about workflow edge cases the design didn't account for
- Distrust may reflect valid concerns about accuracy or job impact
- Slow adoption may signal poor UX or inadequate training

Treat resistance as a feedback source, not a compliance problem.

---

## Deployment Approach

### Phased Rollout

Prefer phased rollout over big-bang deployment:

1. **Closed pilot** — small group, close monitoring
2. **Expanded pilot** — broader group, structured feedback
3. **Staged rollout** — department by department
4. **Full deployment** — with lessons from prior phases applied

Each phase produces feedback that improves the next.

### Rollback Plan

Define what a rollback looks like before deploying. If the AI system produces unacceptable outcomes, can the organization return to the previous process? If not, the risk profile is higher.

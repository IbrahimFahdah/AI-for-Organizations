# ROI and Value

## Overview

AI ROI is frequently overstated in projections and understated in post-mortems. The measurement problem is real: AI value often shows up in ways that are difficult to attribute — improved decision quality, reduced cognitive load, avoided errors — rather than in clean cost line items.

---

## Defining Value

Before measuring ROI, define what value means for a specific use case:

| Value Type | Examples | Measurability |
|---|---|---|
| Cost reduction | Fewer FTEs on a task, reduced vendor spend | High |
| Time savings | Faster task completion, shorter cycle times | High |
| Quality improvement | Fewer errors, higher accuracy | Medium |
| Revenue impact | Better conversion, reduced churn | Medium |
| Risk reduction | Fewer compliance violations, better audit trails | Low |
| Decision quality | Better-informed choices at scale | Low |

Measure the types that are measurable. Acknowledge those that are not without fabricating numbers.

---

## Measurement Approach

### Baseline First

Measure the current state before deploying AI. Without a baseline, before/after comparisons are impossible.

Key baseline metrics:
- Task completion time
- Error rate or rework rate
- Cost per unit of output
- Volume handled per FTE

### Controlled Comparison

Where possible, compare AI-assisted groups to non-AI control groups. This is harder than it sounds in practice but produces more reliable attribution.

### Time Horizon

AI ROI is rarely immediate. Typical timelines:

- **0-3 months:** Setup, training, low adoption
- **3-9 months:** Adoption grows, early productivity gains visible
- **9-18 months:** Process integration, measurable efficiency gains
- **18+ months:** Compounding returns as usage matures

Projects evaluated at 6 months frequently look like failures that would succeed at 18 months.

---

## Common Measurement Mistakes

- **Counting capacity freed, not cost reduced.** If AI saves 10 hours/week per employee, the value depends on what those hours are used for — not just the number of hours.
- **Vendor-provided ROI calculators.** These are sales tools, not analysis.
- **Double-counting.** Multiple teams claiming the same efficiency gain.
- **Ignoring implementation cost.** Total cost of ownership includes integration, training, monitoring, and ongoing maintenance.

---

## Total Cost of Ownership

| Cost Component | Examples |
|---|---|
| Licensing/API costs | Vendor subscription, token consumption |
| Implementation | Engineering time, integration work |
| Operations | Monitoring, incident response, updates |
| Training | User training, change management |
| Governance | Risk review, compliance, audit |
| Opportunity cost | What else could be built/bought |

# Chapter 27: Industry and Public Sector Case Studies

> *This chapter will be populated with documented case studies. Templates are provided for each sector below.*

---

## Government Transformation

**Pattern:** Document processing, citizen services, internal knowledge management.

**Common deployment:** AI-assisted processing of permit applications, benefits claims, or policy research — where high volume and standardized inputs create good conditions for automation.

**Key challenge:** Procurement timelines, transparency requirements, and equity obligations mean government deployments often have stricter governance requirements than private sector equivalents.

**Lesson pattern:** Start with internal-facing use cases to build organizational confidence before deploying toward citizens. Pilot with a single agency or function before enterprise rollout.

---

## Healthcare Organizations

**Pattern:** Clinical documentation, decision support, administrative automation.

**Common deployment:** AI scribes that generate clinical notes from recorded conversations, reducing documentation burden on clinicians.

**Key challenge:** Regulatory requirements (HIPAA, FDA guidance on clinical decision support), liability for clinical errors, and clinician trust.

**Lesson pattern:** Clinical documentation has lower risk than clinical decision support. Deploy documentation assistance first; validate rigorously before any system that influences clinical decisions.

---

## Banking and Finance

**Pattern:** Fraud detection, document analysis, customer service, risk assessment.

**Common deployment:** AI-assisted document review for loan origination, KYC, or contract analysis — high volume, structured documents with clear acceptance criteria.

**Key challenge:** Model risk management requirements (SR 11-7 equivalent), fair lending compliance, and explainability requirements for adverse decisions.

**Lesson pattern:** Generative AI for document summarization and drafting has lower regulatory friction than AI in credit decisions. Build compliance infrastructure for the latter before deploying.

---

## Retail and Logistics

**Pattern:** Demand forecasting, personalization, route optimization, customer service.

**Common deployment:** AI-powered customer service triage that handles common inquiries and routes complex cases to agents — reduces handle time and improves consistency.

**Key challenge:** Integration with existing commerce and logistics platforms; managing customer expectations when AI is wrong.

**Lesson pattern:** Measure customer satisfaction before and after AI deployment, not just cost metrics. Customers who encounter AI errors and feel poorly handled have higher churn.

---

## Technology Enterprises

**Pattern:** Developer productivity, internal knowledge management, product AI features.

**Common deployment:** Internal developer tools (code completion, documentation generation, code review assistance) as the first AI deployment, before customer-facing features.

**Key challenge:** Developer adoption culture — engineers have strong opinions about tooling and low tolerance for tools that slow them down.

**Lesson pattern:** Let developers choose and evaluate tools within a governed set rather than mandating a single tool. Adoption is higher when developers feel ownership.

## Case Study Template

For organizations contributing case studies to this guide:

```markdown
## [Organization Type], [Industry]

**Problem:** What specific problem was AI deployed to solve?
**Scale:** Organization size, affected user count, volume of work.
**Solution:** AI approach, architecture, integration.
**Timeline:** From pilot decision to production.
**Results:** Before/after metrics on primary outcome.
**What worked:** Top 2-3 factors in success.
**What didn't:** Top 2-3 challenges or failures.
**What we'd do differently:** Honest retrospective.
```

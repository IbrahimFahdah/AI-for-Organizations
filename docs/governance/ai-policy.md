# AI Policy

## Overview

An AI policy defines the rules governing how AI systems are used within an organization. It provides employees with clear guidance, creates accountability, and reduces legal and reputational risk.

---

## Why a Policy is Necessary

Without a policy:
- Employees make individual judgments about acceptable AI use with no shared standard
- Sensitive data flows into unvetted AI tools
- The organization has no grounds for consistent enforcement
- Regulatory requirements may be violated unknowingly

---

## Policy Scope

An effective AI policy covers:

### Approved Tools

- Which AI tools are approved for use, and for what purposes
- How employees request access to new AI tools
- What tools are explicitly prohibited

### Data Handling

- What categories of data may be sent to external AI tools
- What data is prohibited (PII, regulated data, confidential business information)
- How to identify data classification before using AI tools

### Output Use

- How AI outputs should be labeled or disclosed
- When human review is required before using AI outputs
- What outputs should never be used without verification

### Acceptable Use

- Prohibited use cases (discrimination, deception, policy violations)
- Requirements for accuracy and verification before publishing AI-assisted content
- Requirements for disclosure to customers or external parties

### Accountability

- Who is responsible for AI-generated outputs
- How errors or harms should be reported
- What the consequences of policy violations are

---

## Policy Development

### Involve Stakeholders

A policy written only by legal or IT will not reflect operational realities. Include:
- Representatives from business units who use AI
- Legal and compliance
- IT and security
- HR (for employment-related questions)

### Keep It Maintainable

AI capabilities and risks evolve fast. Write a policy that:
- Establishes principles, not exhaustive tool lists
- Has a defined review cadence (minimum annual, ideally quarterly)
- Has an owner who is responsible for updates

### Make It Accessible

Policies that are long, legalistic, and hard to find are not followed. Supplement the formal policy with:
- Plain-language summaries by role
- Quick reference cards
- Decision trees for common questions

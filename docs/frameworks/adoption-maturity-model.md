# Adoption Maturity Model

## Overview

The AI Adoption Maturity Model describes the progression from initial experimentation to scaled, governed AI deployment. It provides a common vocabulary for assessing where an organization is and what the next stage requires.

---

## Maturity Levels

### Level 1 — Exploration

**Description:** AI is used informally by individuals. No organizational policy, standard tools, or shared learnings.

**Characteristics:**
- Individual employees use consumer AI tools (ChatGPT, Copilot, etc.)
- No approved tool list
- No data handling guidance
- No shared standards or practices

**Risks:** Shadow AI, data leakage, inconsistent quality.

**Next step:** Define a basic AI policy. Identify and approve initial tools. Launch a structured pilot.

---

### Level 2 — Piloting

**Description:** Structured pilots in specific functions. Approval process exists. Results are measured.

**Characteristics:**
- Approved tool list established
- Pilots in one or more business units
- Basic data handling guidance in place
- Pilot results documented and shared

**Risks:** Pilot purgatory — pilots succeed but nothing scales.

**Next step:** Define scale criteria. Secure budget and resources for production deployment. Establish governance framework.

---

### Level 3 — Deployment

**Description:** AI is deployed in production in specific use cases. Governance, monitoring, and support are in place.

**Characteristics:**
- Production deployments with real users
- Governance process for approving new use cases
- Output quality monitoring in place
- Training and support available for users
- ROI measurement in progress

**Risks:** Inconsistent quality across deployments. Governance not keeping pace with adoption.

**Next step:** Systematize governance. Build shared infrastructure. Expand use cases based on lessons learned.

---

### Level 4 — Scaling

**Description:** AI is deployed across multiple functions with shared infrastructure, governance, and measurement.

**Characteristics:**
- Shared AI platform or gateway
- Cross-functional governance committee
- Systematic evaluation and quality standards
- AI investment tied to business outcomes
- Formal change management for new deployments

**Risks:** Complexity increasing faster than governance capacity. Technical debt in early deployments.

**Next step:** Drive continuous improvement. Develop organizational AI competency. Address technical debt.

---

### Level 5 — Optimizing

**Description:** AI is embedded in core operations. Continuous improvement of AI systems is systematic. AI literacy is broad.

**Characteristics:**
- AI integral to key business processes
- Feedback loops from operations to AI improvement
- Broad AI literacy across the organization
- Governance mature and efficient
- AI differentiation is a competitive asset

---

## Maturity by Dimension

| Dimension | Level 1 | Level 2 | Level 3 | Level 4 | Level 5 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Policy | None | Basic | Established | Comprehensive | Adaptive |
| Governance | None | Ad hoc | Defined | Systematic | Continuous |
| Data | Unmanaged | Assessed | Controlled | Governed | Optimized |
| Engineering | None | Prototypes | Production | Platform | Automated |
| People | Informal | Trained | Adopted | Proficient | Expert |

# Chapter 5: Creating an AI Vision

An AI vision defines what the organization is trying to become through AI — not what tools it will buy. Without it, AI investment fragments into disconnected projects that do not compound.

---

## What a Vision Does

A useful AI vision:
- Connects AI investment to organizational mission and strategic goals
- Gives teams a shared direction to align toward
- Helps prioritize among competing use cases
- Creates executive accountability for outcomes

A poor AI vision: "We will be an AI-first organization leveraging cutting-edge technology to transform our operations." This says nothing actionable.

---

## Defining Organizational Ambition

Before writing a vision, answer three questions:

1. **What are the 2-3 most important strategic outcomes for the organization in the next 3 years?**
2. **Where does AI create the most leverage toward those outcomes?**
3. **What would need to be true for AI to deliver that leverage?**

The answers constrain the vision to something achievable rather than aspirational.

---

## AI-Driven Business Models

| Model | Description | Organizational Implication |
|---|---|---|
| Cost efficiency | AI reduces operational cost | Process redesign, headcount planning |
| Revenue growth | AI improves product or sales outcomes | Product and engineering investment |
| New services | AI enables offerings that did not exist before | R&D, go-to-market strategy |
| Data monetization | AI extracts value from proprietary data | Data governance, partnerships |

Most organizations start with cost efficiency. The others require more organizational change.

---

## Strategic Transformation Goals

An AI vision translates into 3-5 transformation goals — specific, time-bounded outcomes. Examples:

- "Reduce document processing time by 60% within 18 months"
- "Enable self-service answers to 80% of tier-1 support queries by Q3"
- "Deploy AI-assisted decision support for all credit decisions by end of year"

Vague goals cannot be evaluated. Specific goals create accountability.

---

## Executive Alignment

A vision without executive alignment is a document. Alignment requires:

- Agreement on what the organization is *not* going to do with AI (scope control)
- Shared understanding of realistic timelines and investment required
- A single executive who owns delivery and is accountable for results

---

## Key Takeaways

- A vision without specificity is not a strategy — it is a press release.
- Ambition should be constrained by what the organization can actually execute.
- Executive alignment on scope and accountability comes before any project work.

**Common mistake:** Writing an AI vision for the board presentation without connecting it to operational reality.

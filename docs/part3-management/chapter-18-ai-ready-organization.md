# Chapter 18: Building an AI-Ready Organization

An AI-ready organization does not just deploy AI tools — it builds the structural capability to adopt AI continuously as the technology and competitive landscape evolve.

---

## AI Operating Models

How AI work is organized varies by organizational maturity and scale:

| Model | Structure | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Federated** | Each business unit manages its own AI | Diverse needs, autonomous units |
| **Centralized** | Central AI team serves all units | Consistency, governance, small orgs |
| **Hub and spoke** | Central CoE + embedded BU capability | Scale, balance of governance and agility |
| **Platform** | Central platform team; BUs build on it | Large enterprises, high volume |

Most organizations start centralized and evolve toward hub-and-spoke as they scale.

---

## Centers of Excellence

An AI Center of Excellence provides shared services to business units:

- Common platforms, tooling, and infrastructure
- Governance and standards
- Reusable components (RAG pipelines, evaluation frameworks, integrations)
- Expertise on demand
- Training and enablement

A CoE that only produces policies and frameworks without operational support will be ignored.

---

## Organizational Structures

AI capability requires roles that most organizations are still building:

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| AI Product Manager | Use case definition, evaluation, adoption |
| AI Engineer | Building and operating AI systems |
| ML/Data Scientist | Model selection, evaluation, fine-tuning |
| AI Risk / Governance Lead | Policy, compliance, oversight |
| Business AI Lead (per BU) | Roadmap, adoption, local governance |

Not every organization needs all of these. Start with the roles that address the biggest gap.

---

## Scaling AI Across Departments

Scaling requires:

- Shared infrastructure (not every team rebuilds the same pipeline)
- Reusable governance (not every use case reviewed from scratch)
- Shared evaluation standards (consistent quality expectations)
- Knowledge sharing (what worked, what failed)

The bottleneck at scale is usually governance and evaluation capacity, not engineering.

---

## Continuous Transformation

AI is not a project with a completion date. Organizations that treat it as ongoing capability building:

- Review the AI portfolio and roadmap quarterly
- Update governance policy annually (or when regulations change)
- Invest in AI literacy continuously, not in one-time training programs
- Learn from both successes and failures systematically

---

## Key Takeaways

- Organizational structure should match scale and maturity; evolve it rather than locking in early.
- A CoE that only governs without enabling will be bypassed.
- At scale, governance and evaluation capacity are usually the constraint, not engineering.

**Common mistake:** Designing the final-state operating model before having enough operational experience to know what is actually needed.
